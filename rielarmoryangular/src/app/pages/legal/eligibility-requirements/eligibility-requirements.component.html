Eligibility Requirements

Not everyone can legally own or possess a firearm in the Philippines. Applicants must meet strict criteria regarding age, mental and physical fitness, criminal background, and citizenship. This section outlines the requirements set by the PNP and Firearms and Explosives Office (FEO) to ensure that only qualified individuals are granted licenses, promoting safety, responsibility, and compliance with national laws.

Minimum Age

The applicant must be at least 21 years old.

Citizenship

The applicant must be a Filipino citizen.

Good Moral Character

Applicants must have no criminal record and possess good moral character, supported by police clearance or certificate of good conduct.

Mental and Physical Fitness

Applicants must undergo and pass a psychological and medical evaluation certifying mental stability and physical fitness.

No Pending Criminal Cases

Applicants must not have any pending criminal cases, especially those involving firearms, explosives, or violent crimes.

No Substance Abuse

Applicants must not be habitual users of illegal drugs or alcohol.

No Prior Administrative Violations

Previous violations related to firearms, explosives, or other regulatory rules may disqualify the applicant.

Financial or Income Proof

Applicants may need to provide proof of stable employment or financial capacity to legally own a firearm.

Proof of Residence

Applicants must show valid proof of residence within the relevant jurisdiction or locality.

Specific License Conditions

Additional documents may be required depending on firearm type (pistol, rifle, light weapon) or purpose (self-defense, sport, collection).
