Licenses and Permits

The Licenses and Permits section explains the various types of firearm licenses and permits required in the Philippines. Understanding these requirements is crucial for legal ownership, safe handling, and compliance with national regulations. This section will guide you through the necessary documentation, eligibility criteria, and processes to ensure responsible and lawful possession of firearms.

License to Own and Possess Firearms (LTOPF)

The primary owner license required under RA 10591. Demonstrates legal entitlement to own and possess specified firearms and is required before purchase or transfer.

Obtaining the appropriate LTOPF type ensures compliance with Philippine laws, supports responsible firearm ownership, and prevents unauthorized access to restricted firearms.

For Types 1 to 5 licenses, a vault or a container secured by lock and key or other security measures for the safekeeping of firearms shall be required.

For Types 3 to 5 licenses, the citizen must comply with the inspection and bond requirements.

LTOPF Type I

Covers handguns such as pistols and revolvers intended for personal protection, sport shooting, or recreational purposes. This is the most commonly issued LTOPF for civilian citizens.

Allows to own and possess a maximum of two (2) registered firearms.

LTOPF Type II

Includes rifles, carbines, and shotguns used for hunting, sporting competitions, or personal defense. Applicants must demonstrate proficiency and a valid justification for ownership.

Allows to own and possess a maximum of five (5) registered firearms.

LTOPF Type III

Pertains to light weapons such as submachine guns or automatic firearms, generally issued to government officials, security agencies, or law enforcement personnel. Civilians are typically restricted from this category.

Allows to own and possess a maximum of ten (10) registered firearms.

LTOPF Type IV

Authorizes the possession of firearms for collectors, museums, or antique firearm enthusiasts. The firearms covered are usually of historical or rare significance.

Allows to own and possess a maximum of fifteen (15) registered firearms.

LTOPF Type V

Reserved for special purposes such as importation, manufacturing, or commercial sale of firearms and ammunition. This type is issued to licensed dealers, importers, and manufacturers regulated by the Firearms and Explosives Office (FEO).

Allows to own and possess more than fifteen (15) registered firearms.

Permit to Carry Firearm Outside of Residence (PTCFOR)

A separate written authorization permitting a licensed individual to carry a firearm outside their home for a specified purpose and duration. It is distinct from ownership.

Permit to Transport Firearm

Issued to authorize movement of a firearm between specified locations for a defined purpose and timeframe, typically required when transporting firearms for sale, repair, or official duties.

Certificate of Registration / Firearm Registration (Short & Long Certificates)

Official registration document issued by the Firearms & Explosives Office (FEO) that records a firearm under an owner’s name. Short and long certificates reflect differing use-cases (e.g., government issue vs. private ownership).

FEO Application Forms

The set of official forms required by the Firearms & Explosives Office for licensing, registration, transfer, and other transactions. These include application forms, affidavits, and declarations required for processing.

Background Clearance / Police Clearance

Local police or national clearances that demonstrate absence of disqualifying criminal records; commonly required as part of LTOPF and registration applications.

Neuro-Psychiatric Clearance

A medical/psychiatric certification from an accredited practitioner or facility confirming the applicant meets mental fitness requirements for firearm ownership.

Drug Test Certificate

Proof of a negative drug screening from a PNP-accredited laboratory or facility, often required when applying for LTOPF or related permits.

Gun Safety / Responsible Ownership Seminar Certificate

Evidence of completion of an approved safety training course; many applications require proof that the applicant has attended a recognized gun-safety seminar.

Proof of Identity and Citizenship (IDs, PSA Birth Certificate, Passport)

Government-issued identification and proof of citizenship (PSA birth certificate or passport) required to verify identity and eligibility for licensing and registration.

Proof of Bill / Residential Address

Documents such as utility bills or official statements used to confirm the applicant’s residence, a standard requirement for LTOPF and related filings.

Proof of Income / Occupation

Employer certificates, pay slips, or other documents evidencing livelihood or occupation, sometimes requested to assess applicant stability and suitability.

Transfer of Ownership / Deed of Sale

Documentation formalizing the sale or transfer of a firearm between parties; used by the dealer and FEO to update the firearm’s registered owner.

Dealer License / Authorized Dealer Documents

Licenses and registration documents that authorize a person or business to buy, sell, or deal in firearms and ammunition, held by firms regulated by the FEO.

Importer / Manufacturer Authorization

Official permits issued to entities licensed to import or manufacture firearms, parts, or ammunition in compliance with FEO rules.

Authority to Import / Customs Clearance

Documents required for legally importing firearms and related items, including the PNP import authorization and customs paperwork.

Forfeiture & Disposition Orders

Court or agency orders governing the seizure, forfeiture, or disposition of firearms subject to legal action or evidence handling; administered through the FEO and court system.

Permit for Exhibition, Demonstration, or Public Display

Special permits required to exhibit or publicly display firearms (e.g., gun shows, demonstrations) that impose conditions on handling and security.

Record of Serial Numbers / Ballistics Marking Documents

Documentation that records a firearm’s serial number and identifying markings, used for registration, tracing, and forensic purposes.

Temporary/Lender Permits and Custody Agreements

Paperwork for short-term custody, lending, or transfer of firearms (for training, repair, or loan), often requiring written custodial agreements and transport permits.

Renewal Notices and LTOPF Renewal Documents

Forms and notices used to renew licenses, registrations, or permits before expiry to remain compliant with Philippine law and FEO requirements.
