Definition of Terms

The world of firearms comes with a wide array of specialized terms and legal definitions. Understanding these is essential for every firearm owner, as it ensures proper handling, compliance with Philippine laws, and responsible ownership. This section will clarify commonly used terminology so you can confidently navigate safety, regulations, and best practices.

Accessories

Accessories are parts of a firearm that may enhance or increase its operational efficiency or accuracy but do not constitute any of its major or minor internal components. Examples include, but are not limited to, laser scopes, telescopic sights, and sound suppressors or silencers.

Ammunition

Ammunition refers to a complete unfixed unit consisting of a bullet, gunpowder, cartridge case, and primer, or a loaded shell, for use in any firearm.

Antique Firearm

An antique firearm is any firearm manufactured at least seventy-five (75) years prior to the current date, excluding replicas; certified by the National Museum of the Philippines as a curio or relic; or any firearm whose value derives significantly from rarity, novelty, or historical association.

Arms Smuggling

Arms smuggling is the unauthorized import, export, acquisition, sale, delivery, movement, or transfer of firearms, their parts, components, and ammunition across national borders without proper legal authorization.

Authority to Import

Authority to import is a document issued by the Chief of the Philippine National Police (PNP) authorizing the legal importation of firearms, their parts, ammunition, or components.

Authorized Dealer

An authorized dealer is any person, legal entity, corporation, or partnership licensed by the Firearms and Explosives Office (FEO) of the PNP to engage in the sale of firearms, ammunition, or parts on a wholesale or retail basis.

Authorized Importer

An authorized importer is any person, legal entity, corporation, or partnership licensed by the FEO to import firearms, ammunition, or parts into the Philippines for sale or distribution.

Authorized Manufacturer

An authorized manufacturer is any entity licensed by the FEO to manufacture firearms, ammunition, or parts for sale or distribution.

Confiscated Firearm

A confiscated firearm is a firearm taken into custody by law enforcement agencies such as the PNP, NBI, or PDEA, which must be reported or turned over to the FEO.

Demilitarized Firearm

A demilitarized firearm is a firearm deliberately rendered incapable of firing projectiles.

Duty Detail Order

A duty detail order is a document issued by an employer specifying the disposition of a firearm, including employee name, firearm information, posting duration and location, and authorized custodian.

Firearm

A firearm is any handheld or portable weapon that expels or is designed to expel a projectile through the force of burning gunpowder or other combustion. The barrel, frame, or receiver is considered part of the firearm.

Firearms Information Management System (FIMS)

FIMS is the compilation of all data and information regarding firearm ownership and disposition for record purposes.

Forfeited Firearm

A forfeited firearm is a firearm subject to court-ordered forfeiture or turned over to the FEO due to abandonment, surrender, confiscation, or license revocation.

Gun Club

A gun club is a registered organization accredited by the FEO that promotes responsible and safe firearm ownership for sports, competitions, self-defense, and collection purposes.

Gunsmith

A gunsmith is a licensed individual or entity that repairs, constructs, or assembles firearms and weapons on a per-order basis, not in commercial quantities.

Imitation Firearm

An imitation firearm is a replica or device so similar in appearance to a real firearm that a reasonable person may mistake it for a genuine firearm.

Licensed Citizen

A licensed citizen is a Filipino who meets the qualifications of the Act and has been issued a license to possess or carry firearms outside the residence.

Licensed Juridical Entity

A licensed juridical entity refers to corporations, organizations, businesses, security agencies, or LGUs licensed to own and possess firearms in compliance with the Act.

Light Weapons

Light weapons include Class-A weapons (self-loading pistols, rifles, carbines, submachine guns, assault rifles, light machine guns not exceeding caliber 7.62mm) and Class-B weapons (crew-served or heavier weapons, e.g., machine guns, grenade launchers, portable anti-tank/anti-aircraft weapons under 100mm caliber).

Long Certificate of Registration

A long certificate of registration is a license issued to government agencies or officials to possess firearms for official duties, excluding security guards.

Loose Firearm

A loose firearm is an unregistered, lost, stolen, illegally manufactured, or otherwise unlawfully possessed firearm.

Major Part or Components of a Firearm

Major parts or components include the barrel, slide, frame, receiver, cylinder, or bolt assembly, and any part designed to convert a semi-automatic or burst firearm to fully automatic.

Minor Parts of a Firearm

Minor parts are any components other than major parts necessary to expel a projectile, excluding accessories.

Permit to Carry Firearm Outside of Residence

A written authorization issued by the Chief of the PNP allowing a licensed citizen to carry a registered firearm outside their residence for a specific purpose and duration.

Permit to Transport Firearm

A written authorization issued by the Chief of the PNP or Regional Director allowing a licensed person or entity to transport a firearm from and to a specific location for a defined purpose and duration.

Residence

The place or places of abode of a licensed citizen as indicated in their firearm license.

Shooting Range

A shooting range is a facility registered and accredited by the FEO for firearms training, testing, and competition, either for members or the public.

Small Arms

Small arms are firearms primarily designed for individual use, including handguns (pistols or revolvers), rifles, and shotguns capable of firing from the hand or shoulder, generally not fully automatic.

Sports Shooting Competition

A sports shooting competition is a defensive, precision, or practical shooting event authorized by the FEO.

Tampered, Obliterated or Altered Firearm

A firearm whose serial number, identification, or ballistics characteristics have been intentionally tampered, obliterated, or altered without authority.

Thermal Weapon Sight

A battery-operated, uncooled thermal imaging device that amplifies thermal signatures to identify targets in various light and weather conditions, often used with other optical or red dot sights.
