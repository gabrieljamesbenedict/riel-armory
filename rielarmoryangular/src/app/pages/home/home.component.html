The Four Primary Rules of Gun Safety

The Four Primary Rules of Gun Safety form the cornerstone of responsible firearm ownership. Every individual who handles a firearm bears the obligation to internalize these principles and apply them without exception. They serve as the foundation for preventing negligent discharges, avoiding tragic accidents, and maintaining the integrity of both the shooter and the shooting community. By embracing these rules as habitual practice rather than temporary caution, firearm owners uphold not only their own safety but also the safety and confidence of everyone who shares their environment.

Rule No. 1

Always treat every firearm as if it is loaded

Rule No. 2

Never point a firearm at anything you are not willing to shoot

Rule No. 3

Keep your finger off the trigger until your sights are on the target and you are ready to fire

Rule No. 4

Be sure of your target and what is beyond it
